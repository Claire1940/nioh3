## Practical tips to get strong early (without turning it into a grind)
This is where Nioh 3 tends to separate casual dabblers from long-term fans. A few high-value habits:

- **Learn one “safe punish” string** per weapon and stop improvising under pressure.
- **Spend Ki like you spend ammo:** always keep enough to dodge twice after attacking.
- **Build around one win condition** (stagger, burst damage, or survivability), not three half-baked ones.
- **Don’t chase perfect loot early.** Upgrade “good enough” and move forward—your power spikes come from systems, not one magical drop.

## So… is Nioh 3 better than Elden Ring?
Here’s my honest take: **they’re aiming at different dopamine.**

Nioh 3 is “better” if you want:
- Combat depth you can practice like a fighting game
- Loot/build progression that rewards optimization
- Tight, repeatable challenges where improvement is measurable

Elden Ring is “better” if you want:
- A vast world that rewards curiosity and wandering
- A more flexible approach to difficulty through exploration order
- That once-in-a-generation sense of discovery
