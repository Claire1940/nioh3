If you want a playstyle that *never lets enemies breathe*, this **nioh 3 dual swords build** is built for it. The entire point is simple: turn Dual Swords into a constant pressure engine that shreds Ki, forces openings, and cashes out with bursty finishers. This matters because in Nioh-style combat, the team who wins the **Ki war** usually wins the fight—especially against aggressive human bosses.

Below is a **nioh 3 dual swords build** framework you can copy and then tune based on what drops.

## Build Goal and Core Gameplan

Dual Swords reward speed, tight spacing, and “hit-confirm” decision making. You’re not trying to one-shot; you’re trying to **force mistakes**.

Your win condition looks like this:

- **Step 1:** Quick strings to build status + chip Ki  
- **Step 2:** Hard Ki pressure (and safe cancels) until the enemy is winded  
- **Step 3:** Punish window → big finisher / grapple / burst chain  
- **Step 4:** Reset distance, repeat

**Player experience:** most Dual Swords players report that fights get easier when they stop “full committing” to long strings and instead treat combos as **2–4 hit probes** that can end at any time.

Because drops and balance patches can shift what’s “best,” treat these as *priority bands*, not strict commandments.

1. **Heart / Ki-related stat** (whatever your Nioh 3 stat name is for Ki pool + Ki recovery)

- **Mid game:** shift into damage scaling once your Ki loop feels stable.

A practical “feel check”:
- If you’re constantly emptying Ki: raise Ki pool/recovery first.  
- If you’re living but fights take forever: raise scaling second.  
- If you’re dying mid-string: raise survivability or swap armor weight.

## Skills, Stances, and the “Lightning Fast” Rotation

Dual Swords are at their best when you’re **stance-aware** and end strings cleanly.

### Skill priorities (easy wins)
Focus on tools that do at least one of these:
- **High Ki damage per second**
- **Safe enders** (you can stop without getting clipped)
- **Gap closing** (to punish whiffs)
- **Fast start-up** (to interrupt or check aggression)

If you’re unsure what to pick, start with:
- One **bread-and-butter quick combo** you can do in any stance
- One **Ki bully skill** for humans
- One **burst finisher** for winded targets

### Simple, reliable rotation
Use this as a template and adapt to what your moveset names are:

- **Opener:** 2–3 fast hits (build pressure safely)  
- **Pressure:** a Ki-focused skill (don’t over-extend)  
- **Confirm:** if the enemy staggers or Ki dips low, commit to a longer string  
- **Cash out:** finisher / grapple window → back off

**Player experience:** most folks improve immediately when they practice *ending* combos early, then re-entering with a micro-dash. Dual Swords aren’t about “longest combo,” they’re about **continuous advantage**.

## Element Choice: Why Lightning Works (and When It Doesn’t)

Lightning is popular because it *tends* to support aggressive melee: slowing enemies or creating more punish windows (depending on how Nioh 3 applies the status).

That said:
- Some enemies resist or shrug off certain elements.
- You should be ready to pivot to another element if a boss matchup feels awful.

A practical approach:
- Run lightning as your default **tempo element**
- Keep a secondary weapon/imbue option for resistant targets
- Don’t tunnel: the best element is the one that keeps your **combo loop safe**

## Combat Tips That Actually Move the Needle

Here’s what usually separates “fast hits” from a real **nioh 3 dual swords build** that melts bosses.

- **Treat Ki like ammo.** Never spend the last 20–30% unless you’re sure you get a payoff.
- **Micro-reset often.** Two hits → reposition → two hits. This keeps you safe and keeps pressure high.
- **Attack after their commitment.** Dual Swords punish recovery frames brutally—bait swings, then dash in.
- **Practice one punish string.** When the enemy is winded, you should have an “autopilot” finisher sequence.
- **Learn one defensive answer.** Whether it’s a parry, a deflect, or a dodge angle—have a plan for panic moments.

## FAQ

**How early can I start this setup?**  
Pretty early. The “pressure loop” is mostly skill + decision making. Gear just makes it louder.

**Is lightning mandatory?**  
No. Lightning is a *tempo choice*. If a boss resists it, swap elements and keep the same gameplan.

**What’s the biggest mistake Dual Swords players make?**  
Over-committing. Dual Swords win by stacking small advantages, not by forcing 10-hit strings into every opening.

**Do I need perfect sets for this to work?**  
Not at all. A few quality-of-life rolls (Ki damage, Ki recovery, status synergy) will usually outperform “random damage” early on.
