## Core combat loop: tag → reposition → burst → reset

Ninja Style shines when you run a simple loop and repeat it until the enemy falls over.

### 1) Tag (create advantage safely)
“Tagging” means applying pressure without committing to a long animation. Think quick throws, short pokes, and low-risk openers.

- Open with a **fast hit** to test reactions.
- Use **ranged ninjutsu** (kunai/shuriken-style tools) to force movement or punish whiffs.
- Aim to apply **status** early (poison/paralysis-style effects, depending on what’s available to you).

### 2) Reposition (take the angle)
After the tag, move to a better line—behind, off to the weapon side, or out of grab range.

- Dodge **through** attacks when the timing is consistent.
- Dodge **away** when the move has a lingering hitbox or a delayed follow-up.
- Don’t circle endlessly—take one clean angle, then act.

### 3) Burst (cash out on a real opening)
Your burst should be short, repeatable, and easy to stop if the enemy recovers.

A solid “default burst” looks like:
- 2–4 hits max
- One finisher or strong hit
- Immediate disengage (or a stance/skill cancel if you’re comfortable)

### 4) Reset (leave before you’re forced out)
This is the big one. Ninja Style wins by **leaving early**.

- Back off when your stamina/Ki (or equivalent resource) dips into danger.
- Re-tag from safety.
- Repeat.

> **Community feedback:** Players consistently say their death rate drops when they treat every burst as “renting the opening,” not owning it.

## Ninjutsu loadout: a flexible “mission kit” you can copy

Instead of one rigid loadout, build a kit that answers common problems: “I need an opener,” “I need a punish,” “I need an escape,” “I need a boss plan.”

### The 4-slot mindset
1) **Opener tool**: something fast that starts your loop  
2) **Punish tool**: something that hits hard when the enemy whiffs  
3) **Control tool**: something that creates breathing room (status, stagger, or space control)  
4) **Safety tool**: something that helps you reset (escape, defense, or emergency heal support)

### Example kits (player experience)
- **Boss learning kit**
  - Opener: quick throw
  - Punish: high-damage single projectile
  - Control: status applicator (poison/paralysis-style)
  - Safety: escape/defense utility

- **Human enemy kit**
  - Opener: quick throw to interrupt
  - Punish: burst tool to cash out during recovery
  - Control: something that stresses Ki/guard (if your kit supports it)
  - Safety: reposition utility

- **Yokai-style enemy kit**
  - Opener: safe poke + distance tool
  - Punish: hard-hitting tool for long animations
  - Control: status or stagger support
  - Safety: “get out” button (because grabs happen)

## Technique upgrades: the 7 habits of an “unfair” shinobi

These are the small habits that change your win rate more than any single piece of gear.

- **You disengage at 60–70% resource, not 10%.** Leaving early is the Ninja tax you gladly pay.
- **You stop comboing into armor.** If the enemy doesn’t flinch, your “burst” is over.
- **You punish the recovery, not the attack.** Attacks are scary; recoveries are free.
- **You keep one tool for “confidence damage.”** Something you can land even when nervous.
- **You pick one status and commit per mission.** Split focus often means no payoff.
- **You practice one “boss-safe burst.”** Same 2–4 hits every time, no improvisation needed.
- **You review deaths like a checklist.** Was it greed, resource panic, camera/spacing, or unfamiliar move?
