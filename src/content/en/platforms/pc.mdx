## How to Optimize Nioh 3 on PC

Once you’ve ensured that your system meets the requirements, the next step is optimizing your settings to achieve the best performance and visuals for your setup. Here are some tips that can help you fine-tune your experience.

### 1. Adjust Graphics Settings for Optimal Performance

- **Resolution**: Playing at 1080p is a safe bet for most mid-range systems. If your PC can handle it, go for 1440p or 4K, but be prepared to adjust settings like shadows and texture quality.
- **Frame Rate**: Aim for a stable 60 FPS for a smooth experience. Disable V-Sync if you prefer lower input latency, but keep in mind this may cause screen tearing.
- **Anti-Aliasing**: Set this to a moderate level, such as FXAA or TAA, to smooth out jagged edges without drastically impacting performance.
- **Texture Quality**: If you're running *Nioh 3* on a system with less VRAM, lowering the texture quality can give a noticeable performance boost without sacrificing too much visual fidelity.

### 2. Enable DLSS (Deep Learning Super Sampling)

If you’re using an Nvidia RTX card, enable **DLSS** to improve frame rates while maintaining visual quality. This AI-based technology helps render your game at a lower resolution and then upscale it to your screen's native resolution, offering a performance boost without a major loss in image quality.

### 3. Update Drivers and System Software

Before diving into the game, ensure that your GPU drivers are up-to-date. Both Nvidia and AMD regularly release updates that optimize performance for new games. Additionally, make sure your system's Windows updates are installed to avoid compatibility issues with *Nioh 3*.

## Troubleshooting Common PC Performance Issues

Even with the right hardware and settings, some *Nioh 3* players may encounter performance issues. Here are some common solutions to help you overcome them.

### Stuttering and Frame Drops

- **Solution**: Lower graphics settings or disable any background applications that may be using system resources.
- **Solution**: Try using a **frame rate cap** to prevent fluctuations in performance, especially if you’re playing at a high frame rate.

### Input Lag or High Latency

- **Solution**: Make sure V-Sync is turned off, as it can cause input lag. Additionally, using **G-Sync** or **FreeSync** (if your monitor supports it) can improve responsiveness and eliminate screen tearing.

## Nioh 3 PC vs Console Versions: Performance Comparison

While *Nioh 3* has made its mark on consoles, the PC version offers better customization in terms of graphical settings and frame rate. Players can expect higher frame rates and better resolution, especially with the support of high-end hardware like the Nvidia RTX series. If you have a powerful system, playing *Nioh 3* on PC offers the ultimate performance, especially with DLSS enabled and settings tweaked for your setup.

For those using **Steam Deck**, the game still runs decently, though you may need to adjust the graphics settings to maintain a steady frame rate. Players can also optimize the game's settings to get a comfortable experience with this portable PC.
