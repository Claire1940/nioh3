After spending 100 hours in the action-packed world of *Nioh 3*, the question on everyone's mind is: "Is Nioh 3 worth it?" If you're a fan of challenging gameplay, deep combat mechanics, and a gripping storyline, then this might be the game for you. But, is it truly worth the time and money? Let’s dive into an honest review after extensive playtime, examining the game’s strengths and weaknesses.

## The Game’s Premise and Storyline

*Nioh 3* carries on the franchise's tradition of blending historical fiction with supernatural elements. Set in the Sengoku period of Japan, you take on the role of a powerful warrior combating terrifying yokai and engaging in epic battles. While the story is familiar, it’s still immersive enough to keep you engaged.

The storyline, though deep and filled with twists, isn’t groundbreaking. The characters are compelling, but the plot sometimes feels like it's rehashing old ideas from previous games in the franchise. That said, for those who enjoy a good action narrative mixed with folklore, it will still appeal.

## Is Nioh 3 Worth It for Combat Lovers?

If there’s one thing that *Nioh 3* does better than most games in its genre, it’s combat. The game’s combat mechanics are top-notch, with multiple stances and weapon types that cater to different playstyles. Whether you prefer the fast-paced ninja combat or the brutal power of samurai weapons, the game gives you plenty of options to experiment with.

However, this complexity also presents a learning curve, making it somewhat inaccessible for newcomers. Combat requires precision, strategic thinking, and a solid understanding of the many systems at play. According to players' feedback, mastering the combat can be both frustrating and rewarding. So, if you’re up for the challenge, *Nioh 3* offers some of the most satisfying combat mechanics in any action RPG.

## The Open World: A Blessing or a Curse?

While *Nioh 3* continues the franchise's tradition of tightly-knit levels, the game introduces open-world segments that give players more freedom. This move is exciting for some, but it may also be a double-edged sword.

Many players feel that the open-world design doesn’t fully capture the essence of *Nioh*'s previous levels. Some areas feel barren and lack the intricate design fans have come to love. However, others appreciate the extra exploration and side missions the world offers, especially with the return of various dynamic events.

For those who enjoy exploring and tackling challenges in an open-world setting, this is a solid feature. But, if you're looking for the level-based precision of previous *Nioh* titles, this shift might be jarring.

## Nioh 3’s Difficulty: A Challenge for Hardcore Gamers

As expected from a game in the *Nioh* franchise, the difficulty is unforgiving. Whether you’re battling a swarm of yokai or taking on massive bosses, the game’s brutal challenge is something that defines its gameplay. According to players' experiences, it’s not for the faint of heart. You’ll find yourself dying often, but with each defeat comes an opportunity to learn and adapt.

For many, the difficulty is a major draw. It’s rewarding when you finally defeat a boss after multiple attempts, and that sense of achievement is what keeps many coming back for more. But for casual gamers or those looking for an easier experience, *Nioh 3* might feel overly punishing.

## Customization and Progression: Plenty of Options

When it comes to character customization, *Nioh 3* offers plenty of depth. You can fine-tune your character’s abilities, stats, and weapons to fit your playstyle. Whether you prefer a stealthy ninja approach or a brute-force samurai build, the game allows for significant freedom in how you progress.

Additionally, the loot system is robust. You can find various gear and weapons that drastically alter your combat experience. This progression system encourages exploration and experimentation, which keeps the gameplay feeling fresh even after hours of play.

## Player Feedback: What the Community Thinks

The general consensus from the community is that *Nioh 3* is a game that rewards persistence. However, its steep learning curve and punishing difficulty have left mixed feelings among players. Some have fallen in love with its complex combat system and challenge, while others have found themselves frustrated by the grind and difficulty spikes.

Ultimately, the community agrees that *Nioh 3* excels in offering deep and rewarding gameplay, but it’s not a game for everyone. According to online discussions and reviews, *Nioh 3* is a love-it-or-hate-it experience.

## Final Verdict: Is Nioh 3 Worth It?

After 100 hours of intense gameplay, the answer to whether *Nioh 3* is worth it ultimately comes down to what you’re looking for in a game. If you crave challenging combat, deep character customization, and a rich (if somewhat familiar) storyline, then *Nioh 3* is definitely worth your time. 

However, if you're someone who prefers a more casual, laid-back experience, or if you're not ready for the punishing difficulty, then you might want to pass on this one.

Overall, *Nioh 3* is a fantastic title for hardcore action RPG fans, but it’s not for the faint-hearted.
